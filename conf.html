Digitalization and innovation as a driver of Sole proprietorship in Ghana

Abstarct

Sole proprietorship plays an integral role for entrepreneurial growth in developing countries despite limited access to funding and undermined capacity for capital accumulation. Sole proprietors in attempt to sustain business growth will require strategic plan that cost less yet impactful particularly for sales and capacity utilization; and business digitalization and innovation is a suggested opportunity. This suggestion has received support from United Nations Commission on Trade and Development which emphasized that Small Medium Enterprises have the opportunity to use digital platforms to explore new market niches and operate at the most competitive level. In fact, digitalization is increasingly becoming the distinguishing factor as to which business gets the edge over the other with access to information, logistics and finance being definitive of competitiveness. Evidently, the benefits of digitalization and innovation are well documented by researchers with a biased focus on manufacturing sector in developed countries where opposing conclusions are drawn. Remaining a puzzle to a large extent is the question of whether benefits are more likely to result from concentration of investment in a narrow stream of digital technologies or diversifying digital technologies using different platforms. An early attempt in understanding the latter is made in this paper for sole proprietors.

Employing World bank Enterprise Survey data (2013) of Ghana, this research assessed the potential of digitalization and innovation as a driver of sole proprietorship by exploring the possibility of integrating these strategies for greater business impact. Spearman rho correlation results indicated a positive relationship among level of digitalization, level of innovation, capacity utilization and annual sales. Mann Whittey U test concluded that sole proprietors who undertook innovation in product, marketing, distribution and logistics earned higher annual sales although difference was insignificant. Insights gained suggest it s possible to integrate digitalization and innovation as a driver of sole proprietorship growth.
